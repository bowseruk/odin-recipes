Chicken Tagine

Description

This dish is named after the clay pot it is cooked in. It is a Morroccan one-pot meal typically served with cous cous.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 8 skinless boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons of grated fresh root ginger
  • pinch of saffron or tumeric
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 400g carrot, cut into sticks
  • small bunch of parsley, roughly chopped
  • lemon wedges
  • 150 ml of water

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a large, wide pan with a lid.
  2. Add the ginger and onion to the pan for 2 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken to the pan and cook until it is lightly coloured.
  4. Add 150 ml of water, the saffron, honey and the carrots to the pan.
  5. Season the mix and stir thoughly.
  6. Bring to the boil and cover and leave to simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken is tender.
  7. Uncover and heat at a high temperature for 5 minutes until the sauce has reduced.
  8. Sprinkle with parsley and serve with lemon wedges
